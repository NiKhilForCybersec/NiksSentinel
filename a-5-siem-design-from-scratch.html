"If you were tasked with designing a SIEM from scratch for a mid-sized company, how would you approach it?"

Structure your answer through the 5 phases:

  1. Discovery: "First, I'd interview stakeholders to understand compliance requirements (PCI, HIPAA, etc.), identify crown jewel assets, and assess the threat landscape. I'd inventory the environment to estimate log volumes."
  2. Design: "Based on their environment (Microsoft-centric vs. multi-cloud), budget, and team expertise, I'd recommend either Splunk or Sentinel. For a mid-size org, I'd size for approximately [X] GB/day."
  3. Build: "I'd deploy the core infrastructure, configure integrations (ticketing, SOAR), and ensure security hardening before onboarding any data."
  4. Onboard: "I'd prioritize log sources in tiers: Tier 1 (AD, firewall, EDR) for immediate visibility, then expand to email, proxy, cloud. Detection rules would map to MITRE ATT&CK, starting with high-fidelity, low-FP rules."
  5. Operationalize: "Finally, I'd establish SOC processes with clear SLAs, define metrics (MTTD, MTTR, FP rate), and create a continuous improvement cycle including weekly tuning and monthly reviews."
